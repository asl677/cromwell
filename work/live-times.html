Avoid the lines with Live Popular Times on Google

Hit up Maps before you head over to your favorite restaurant, local bar or even airport to get a better sense of crowd volume before making a big mistake.
‍

  • Role

  • Visual Design

  • Interaction Design

  • User Experience

  • Strategy


I worked with a talented motion designer and writer on this project. The majority of the motion work was used for introducing the feature, as well as it's breathing gradient that moves in rotation. My role was to define a scalable UI and User Experience to fit within an existing framework. A lot of folks were already mistaking usual popular times for real-time. We felt appending to that mental model was a good place to start.

Pink provided a neutral, yet urgent color to sit against the varying shades of blue we currently had inside our graph. What it also did was provide a color that could overlay onto other colors like blue and create a clear delineation between the two.

Being able to overlap pink with blue allowed us to show case historical and live data together, without adding bias to why this place is busy or not. It scaled well between both low and high volume crowd data.
