Making it easier to post with

linkedin visibility settings

01

  • 2019 — UX Lead

Linkedin's post visibility settings are unclear & suffer from dated components

Today, it’s not terribly clear how or why a member should switch privacy settings inside the Share box, nor do These settings correlate between entry points or feeds. Clarity and confidence are big pain points with members trying to understand where they are actually sharing content to.

02

We decided to align with design systems & bring clarity to each setting

We don’t currently provide enough info on what our settings actually mean, resulting in mis-use or abandonment of their task. We are currently providing two different experiences depending on which entry point you hit first. When I land on groups it should have their settings set, as well as support several UI types and don’t adhere to Art Deco guidelines.


03

Resulting in framework able to handle core visibility settings, but also emerging — such as groups & events

Ideally, products should leverage shared Art Deco components, eg bottom sheets, styling & behavior. By providing more context & improving product writing, targeting the right audience will improve confidence & lead to the right posts landing in the right feeds, leading to more posts. We found that by using a shared framework and adding clearer copy to existing labels, more members were able to successfully post publicly or into a specific group or a event.
