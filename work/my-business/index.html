My role
Worked with several other designers on this project. We defined the UX strategy alongside PM vision thinking. At first a small team with low visibility, growing to a vast Google-wide effort with commercials around the world.

MVP aimed to break away from the Google+ platform (Plus Business Pages housed all of this content) and consolidate all Google-facing business products into a sinlge suite - known as Google My Business. I defined features, interaction patterns, worked with motion design and a handful of iOS, Android and front-end engineers to create the very first version of this product.

Challenge
Merchants don't have a clear path to manage or promote their business on Google
Back then, 56% of local retailers hadn’t claimed their Google listing. Yelp and Yahoo are close behind and moving fast. So, what opportunities are in this space?

Right now, any attempt is hidden from the user and it’s not obvious where all of this info surfaces once provided. There’s no 1:1 correlation between editing and what’s being shown on Google.

Opportunity + use cases
What are merchants doing with other products?
Other local products such as Yelp & Foursquare (shown) have simpler editors within their consumer apps, but don’t cater to navigation & transit like Google does (less complex, lighter APK’s etc). Most use inline editing per field. Verified merchants have increased control & visibility in Google Search

How do I update my hours of operation?
Data + user flows
A hassle-free, actions-first framework aimed at SMB's - was a great place to start
Verifications needed to increase in order for features to be unlocked, and to utlize the full potential of your free listing. The idea was to make verifcation part of on-boarding and allow users to receive moderation udpates on how to keep their listing running smoothly.

If Google wanted good data, they needed to create an omni-directional tool helping others gain something in return from their investment in providing info.

Outcome
A product aimed at small businesses + collecting accurate data
Ultimately and after multipe rounds of research, we decided to target small businesses and let our desktop sister product handle chains and bulk actions. The mobile approach was aimed at taking advantage of device-specific features (eg mobility and camera), encouraging merchants to update their information and share content no matter their location.

We had successfully traversed away from the social side of Google, and now it was time to set sail on a new experience that wasn't beholden to another platform.
