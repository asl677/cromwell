Manage a business on Google Search & Maps

Online service transactions are growing and Google wants to support them end to end for verticals like dining, fitness and events.

  • Visual Design

  • Interaction Design

  • User Experience

  • Prototyping

  • Strategy

My role was to provide ample visual, user experience and interaction support across all platforms, and between several engineering teams including Android, iOS and web. Nearly all of the micro-interaction design within on-boarding, quick actions, reviews, photos and various forms of creation -- were defined by me, and ultimately used in massive collaboration effort.

Local Merchants and brands didn't have a clear path to manage or promote content. Originally coupled with Google Plus, a merchant's profile was the core of their identity and how they were displayed on Maps, now known as Google My Business. Verified merchants were flatlining and we needed to onboard folks outside of the United States even more so. This included quality data and a relationship between both parties -- Google was actually there to help you grow.

After miles of research inside the US, and other areas such as Brazil and India, a series of web and mobile products acting as a suite was the best route forward. After moving away from the social mentality, Google was able to provide a series of new ways for business owners to get onto Google, for free and actually have a return on the investment. From responding to reviews, to updating and moderating your own listing as it's shown to the world, Google My Business is a tiny, yet powerful tool helping over 1B businesses worldwide today.
