  • 2013 — 1 of several UX leads

Merchants needed to manage their Google listings

My role was to provide ample visual, user experience and interaction support across all platforms, and between several engineering teams including Android, iOS and web. Nearly all of the micro-interaction design within on-boarding, quick actions, reviews, photos and various forms of creation -- were defined by me, and ultimately used in massive collaboration effort. Other designers focused on other tracks like business info management and insights.

Local Merchants and brands didn't have a clear path to manage or promote content. Originally coupled with Google Plus, a merchant's profile was the core of their identity and how they were displayed on Maps, now known as Google My Business. Verified merchants were flatlining and we needed to onboard folks outside of the United States even more so. This included quality data and a relationship between both parties -- Google was actually there to help you grow.

02

We developed a platform to easily manage your info, reviews & ads

Research told us to move out of Google+. Google was then able to provide a series of new ways for business owners to get onto Google, for free and actually have a return on the investment. From responding to reviews, to updating and moderating your own listing as it's shown to the world, Google My Business is a tiny, yet powerful tool helping over 1B businesses worldwide today stay up to date with customers and local features.
