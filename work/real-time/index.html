Challenge
Users don't now how lively places are until they get there
With this launch, we aimed to augment the Popular Times histogram by overlaying the place's real time busyness, thus allowing the user to simultaneously see what's typical for the place at this time, and what's actually happening at the current time. An MVP didn't allow us to redesign our graph or radically change the placepage. This was a fairly set of minimum requirements to start getting feedback on the data. I teamed up with a super talented motion designer to deliver a v1 experience. We then looked at existing and competitive patterns to help determine next steps.
High-level user flow
Opportunity
We found that we could confidently surface real-time crowd info at places
Since we already have a feature that showcases estimated crowd volume, let's start with augmenting this space to somehow show both data types: historical and live.

A user would locate the info in the same location as Popular Times within the Placesheet, after a search.
How busy is the post office during lunch time?
User flows
Airports, DMV's & dining use cases were top needs
Research told us that while red tested the best in terms of awareness, it created a sense of negativity across places and made users feel it wasn't a good time to visit. When in reality, a place being busy has nothing to do with wait or suggesting it isn't a good time to go, it's just how many people are there. Popularity doesn't actually mean awful, and in fact could mean - 'Hey, go now!'. How might we overlap these two pieces of data?

visual design

motion + interaction design
Visibility is tied to the user's scroll, swiping between days shows more times + a pulsing gradient keeps things alive
Outcome
Why pink scaled the best between accessibility + use cases
Ultimately, we decided that pink plus animation would be the best direction forward. All while other colors like white against yellow didn't pass accessibility. This allowed for us to use the existing graph UI, and still passed accessibliity while providing a toned down spectrum of color that could be used between cases. See it featured live on The Tonight Show. Big, big thanks to the talented Matt Denzer for all of his motion design support on this. I also want to thank Ashley Proctor for her amazing writing and differentiation between cases and research.
Take-aways + Next steps
Focus on vertical epxansion and improving data quality through machine learning
Leading with Dining vertical allowed us to set a stage for future verticals to provide real time data as we get it and can confidently display for all places. We'll be looking for new ways to surface even more info, while improving the Popular Times experience overall.
