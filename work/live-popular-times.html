A Scalable UI Surfacing Live Crowd Data Across Google Search & Maps

Using the existing Popular Times Framework, overlay real-time content to better inform users how busy local places are

With this launch, we aimed to augment the Popular Times histogram by overlaying the place's real time busyness, thus allowing the user to simultaneously see what's typical for the place at this time, and what's actually happening at the current time.

My role was to define a scalable UI and User Experience to fit within an existing framework. I teamed up with a super talented motion designer to deliver a v1 experience. We then looked at existing and competitive patterns to help determine next steps. A lot of folks were already mistaking usual popular times for real-time. So, we felt appending to that mental model was a good place to start. The idea was to provide enough contrast between usual and real-time data, so that people start to learn there's a massive difference between the two. But how do we do this in a way that scales for every place, amongst other constraints?

Research told us that while red tested the best in terms of awareness, it created a sense of negativity across places and made users feel it wasn't a good time to visit. When in reality, a place being busy has nothing to do with wait or suggesting it isn't a good time to go, it's just how many people are there. Popularity doesn't actually mean awful, and in fact could mean - 'Hey, go now!'. How might we overlap these two pieces of data?

A lot of users were already en route to a destination, so we also wanted to cater to drivers who needed something to glance at in a shorter amount of time, rather than on foot on while idle. We also didn't have bandwidth to re-invent the graph entirely. But overlaying color and text was a viable option. Ultimately, we decided that pink plus animation would be the best direction forward. All while other colors like white against yellow didn't pass accessibility.
