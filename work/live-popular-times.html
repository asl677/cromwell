Surfacing Live crowd data across Google Search & Maps

  • Day Planning
  • Visual Design
  • Interaction Design
  • User Experience

Using the existing Popular Times Framework, overlay real-time content to better inform users how busy local places are

With this launch, we aimed to augment the Popular Times histogram by overlaying the place's real time busyness, thus allowing the user to simultaneously see what's typical for the place at this time, and what's actually happening at the current time.

I worked with a talented motion designer and writer on this project. The majority of the motion work was used for introducing the feature, as well as it's breathing gradient that moves in rotation. My role was to define a scalable UI and User Experience to fit within an existing framework. A lot of folks were already mistaking usual popular times for real-time. We felt appending to that mental model was a good place to start.

Research told us that while red tested the best in terms of awareness, it created a sense of negativity across places and made users feel it wasn't a good time to visit. When in reality, a place being busy has nothing to do with wait or suggesting it isn't a good time to go, it's just how many people are there. Popularity doesn't actually mean awful, and in fact could mean - 'Hey, go now!'. How might we overlap these two pieces of data?

Pink provided a neutral, yet urgent color to sit against the varying shades of blue we currently had inside our graph. What it also did was provide a color that could overlay onto other colors like blue and create a clear delineation between the two. Being able to overlap pink with blue allowed us to show case historical and live data together, without adding bias to why this place is busy or not. It scaled well between both low and high volume crowd data.
