Access to top categories on Google Maps

  • Visual Design

  • Interaction Design

  • User Experience

  • Prototyping

  • Strategy

In 2015, Google Maps supported categorical shortcuts, but they were hidden beneath the keypad and the list was only so forgiving. Folks inside and outside of the United States wanted quicker access to these needs, not only while on foot, but during a number of driving cases, and they weren't easy to see, easy to touch or easy to remember where each category was in the list.

We took a look at some competitor models (Apple Maps, City Mapper) that tackled similar localization cases as well as both types of navigation, as well as at a similar point of friction Google Maps faced vs others.

After a lot of research and prototyping, syncing with visual design leads and ensuring parity across Maps and it's existing roadmap,

We  landed on an icon set and style that worked well for the key pieces we wanted to tackle -- visibility, consistency and tapability.

Moving to a collapsable, bolder, red icon layout helped align to our existing base maps as well as other zero suggest efforts currently in the works. Try it now on Google Maps.
