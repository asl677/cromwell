Challenge
Brand align + revitalize Navigational Search
Navigational Search refers to specific intent that lands you inside a place (restaurants, hotels, gas stations, national parks and so on). It was time for Google's iconic Place page to get with the times. We set out to define a short to long term vision - and worked through milestones quarter by quarter.

The crux of this effort stemmed from a company-wide effort to standardize and align design across products. It was a long term vision set out to evolve how Google looks and works across it's ecosystem. Various parts of Search and Maps needed to adhere and test alongside their specific use cases. Next to Search, Maps and Local was next on the list to re-think their look.

We decided to iterate and test on these patterns in order to best accomodate the user needs within iOS, Android and mobile web.

For example, iOS and Android were more focused on driving and overall navigation, rather than core actions like calling and booking a table. Slight varations to our actions and heirarchies needed to be weighed, tested and standardized alongside Search's research and North Star vision.

Outcome + UI
We learned a lot + defined a realistic path forward for Local Search
While simple in appearance, this was not a simple task to embark on - and wasn't going to happen overnight. This project took over a year and required a myriad of VP reviews, approvals and cross-functional support, but also awareness of how we planned to own the framework but work with partners to spec out, test and standardize UI as part of the greater goal.
