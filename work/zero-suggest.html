Making it easy to search for & locate top local needs in Google Maps

Users were struggling with query formulation & typing more than they needed to for things like dining & gas

A lot of local's mobile search traffic was coming from top categories like restaurants, ATM's and gas, but it was still difficult for most to find these things with ease. At the time, Google Maps supported categorical shortcuts, but they were hidden beneath the keypad and the list was only so forgiving.

Folks inside and outside of the United States wanted quicker access to these needs, not only while on foot, but during a number of driving cases, and they weren't easy to see, easy to touch or easy to remember where each category was in the list. We took a look at some competitor models (Apple Maps, City Mapper) that tackled similar localization cases as well as both types of navigation, as well as at a similar point of friction Google Maps faced vs others.

I provided overarching visual design, user experience and interaction design support (including the expando pattern you see below), as well as strategy shaping and selling the ultimate vision. After a lot of research and prototyping, syncing with visual design leads and ensuring parity across Maps and it's existing roadmap, we ultimately landed on an icon set and style that worked well for the key pieces we wanted to tackle -- visibility, consistency and tappability, Moving to a bolder, red icon layout helped align to our existing basemaps as well as other zero suggest efforts currently in the works.
