Bookings on google

01

  • 2016 — Lead Product Designer

People want to book reservations & haircuts from Maps

We needed a flexible system to handle our local verticals and potentially 350M places on Google. Leveraging available and competitive patterns, we then prioritized work based on query volume, and how we planned to work with others. We then developed a template flow that could scale between several verticals, to include dining, fitness, beauty/wellness, hotels, events and services. We tested several flows, patterns and multiple rounds of research from surveys to cafe and rapid user studies, using interactive prototypes.

We ultimately decided to evolve our Popular Times feature into a more interactive and robust product serving a series of visit data: Popular Times, Live Popular Times and estimated wait times.

02

Resy, Open Table & Mindbody as platform partners

I worked closely with another designer to create a scalable partner framework, UI kit and lay a foundation for future transactional support. Finding that Icon-driven actions didn't scale well between locales, and didn't allow for customized strings for specific verticals, moving us to a text-only approach for entry points within the place sheet. Taking cues from competitors like Resy and Open Table, we then partnered with products like Mindbody to support and end to end booking experience all within Google.

The sensitivity of this info was also to be considered, as well as how it played a role in decision making between parsing how busy a place was at that very time vs another day of the week. After rounds of research, we also found new ways to allow users to traverse between times as well as focus on the worst case scenario at a glance.
