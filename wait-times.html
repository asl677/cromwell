Challenge

People need help planning their days

Today, we can confidently overlay usual wait times with live and historical crowd volume. Right now, we can surface this info for over 1B dining establishments.

We considered a number of use cases and existing models to help determine the right UI and location to start with. Over 30% of users attempt to interact with the graph bars, and we don't currently provide and data in order for that to be useful.

Let's converge on findings and patterns and see what kind of new experience we can provide.

Research

People tend to want to know the worst possible case, then avoid that time frame

We knew that we at least had the data (to infer a mean wait time average) for over 70% of restaurants listed on Google. Can we create a framework to support more needs over time? Other verticals needed higher confidence rates to be considered.

We decided to focus on immediate and long term dining use cases first - primarily restaurants and grocery stores. How do we support this need alongside historical and live data already within the Popular Times block?

Findings + USER Flows

Pairing with Popular Times was more scalable than with hours of operation

So, we found that most users were not only mistaking popular times for wait times in the first place (some less so than others), and were mentally putting the two data pieces in the same thought.

So, why not find some way to combine the information? We would need to consider live data, historical, and an unbiased representation of wait times between the days and weeks. We tested several patterns through rapid visual design and prototyping methods. I worked closely with a researcher to create a plan and gather usable feedback to adjust the overall direction.

Explorations

We explored several existing + new visual design patterns

A tooltip UI allowed users to traverse hours and showcase a peak wait time for the selected day. Originally, the beak on the tooltip aimed to add more attention to the selected hour, but didn't scale to the edge well.

The summary focused on the worst case scenario for that day. Most users wanted to get a general sense for when not to go, but also wanted the ability to see other times.
