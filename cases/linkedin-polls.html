members across linkedin struggle to gather useful data from their network. many hack the system in order to gather sentiment or opinions on questions they have, typically pertaining to their occupation or career. more often than not, these questions lead to meaningful and engaging conversations.

03

we looked back at past work and then into existing projects, design systems and other use cases. because linkedin was already filled with text heavy content and needed more visual contrast, we led with a more lightweight, visual approach.

04

ultimately, we found shorter character lengths and a quick creation process to scale best. initial product supports up to 4 options and allows for the creator to view votes per option, keeping true to original privacy principles.

this polls product also introduces bits of motion to enhance user delight, as well as cross-platform support across web, ios, and android.
