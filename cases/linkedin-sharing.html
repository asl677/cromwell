A more scalable & usuable linkedin Sharing

Sharing on Linkedin needed framework level improvements, primarily across web experiences. We noticed drop-offs, frustrations and ample usability flaws with existing implementation and set out to course correct.

03

After a massive audit and a look at some past work, we uncovered deep, dark engineering and UX patterns that needed to be lifted. Feeds across Groups, Pages and Hashtags also suffered from usability and shared component support, considering most of sharing was custom code.

Custom code and suffering partner support ensured tech debt and UX challenges not scaling over time. We decided to invest in a complete migration to shared components, working closely with Design Systems, Infra and research to ensure vetted patterns were used across navigation, systems and writing. We broke the work up into phases by quarter and kept partner teams in the loop through out each phase. This has paved a path for the team to further evolve the share box and it's core flows in a more systematic and scalable way.
