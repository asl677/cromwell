A new visual design for Google's local search

modernizing navigational search on google. it was time local search caught up to search's new design language. the goal was to take research and validated system parts such as navigation, imagery and actions, and stress test them against local needs -- including dining, salons and hotel use cases.

I worked specifically on imagery, actions and traversal. We threaded product work with visual in order to meet existing quarterly goals, including infinite scrolling hero imagery, local-friendly actions and a cohesive way to move between pages.

Our team worked closely with the design systems and other pillars like navigation to leverage research, explorations and consistency. I worked within several timezones, from the United States to Tokyo.

We started with our own and existing projects, reshaped their roadmaps, aligned with various local teams, eg shopping, transactions, imagery and ugc. chains and categorical search were already so embedded into core search's framework, it was getting attention already, but nav needed some serious work.

the effort intended to set the stage for modernizing three types of local search  -- categorical, chains and navigational search (essentially place listings across maps and search).
