2016

Bookings across Google Search + Maps

Allowing users on Search or Maps with a Google website to make bookings or purchase tickets with local businesses.

Goals

I led and worked closely with another talented designer on this project.

The goal was to develop a scalable system and set of guidelines for partners and other teams to leverage within the local search framework, that scales between dining, salons, studios, hotels and events.

Creating a framework for first and third party partners to tap into, creating a seamless and transparent experience across places on Google Maps, keeping users within the ecosystem longer and without hardship.

Challenge

Online service transactions are growing + Google wants to support it end to end

That means we should make it possible for users to take actions with local businesses in the real world, right from Google. Initial focus was on a transactions framework: scheduling appointments, classes, making reservations, and so on.

Not only will this generate a new path for customer acquisition, but it also further automates the process of scheduling an appointment online, which means less effort for you and more appointments on your calendar. Also, it’s free.

Research

A lot of traffic was not only coming from dinner reservations, hotel bookings and the need to support haircuts and classes were not only a need for Google but for businesses and 3rd party products.

As stated before, this was a good opportunity to solve a much needed omnidirectional problem for both Google, consumers and merchants.
‍

Let's standardize our framework.

Our existing experiences were disjointed and needed framework investment. Other products like Resy, Open Table shared several patterns for reservations, ultimately driving us to utilize these and other vetted patterns across our places.

We saw an opportunity to provide a platform for customers and merchants to connect and transact between. We found that most merchants wanted to offer these services, but didn't have a platform other than a paid third party. Power yoga users were using Mindbody and Salons were using Square to offer appointment slots.

However, data and time slot selection was a key modifier in most verticals, we just needed a scalable system for it.
‍

Explorations

But we needed a flexible system to handle our verticals.

Leveraging available and competitive patterns, we then prioritized work based on query volume, and how we planned to work with others. We then developed a template flow that could scale between several verticals, to include dining, fitness, beauty/wellness, hotels, events and services. We tested several flows, patterns and multiple rounds of research from surveys to cafe and rapid user studies, using interactive prototypes.

We stress tested entry points + other patterns across partners, verticals + platforms to ensure scalability

Icon-driven actions didn't scale well between locales, and didn't allow for customized strings for specific verticals. We decided text only entry points allowed for more localized-friendly real estate. We then worked with production, writers and marketing to align on scope and land a successful product launch.

Ultimately, our v1 was a mobile-optimized MVP. It currently supports web and mobile web. More platforms and verticals supported over time.
‍

The UX team worked closely with visual design leads and production designers to standardize components into stickersheets, creating a shareable system for other partners and teams to leverage and test against.
