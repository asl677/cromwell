How I Got Here

In 2011, I co-founded a business focusing on B2B products, leading nearly all design efforts

We then moved to San Francisco to make this all happen.

Known as DarwinApps, I worked with everyone from CMO's to videographers. Typically helping out other early stage products, while focusing on our own ideas at the same time. Clients included The Air National Guard, Creative Dash and AT&T. A few things have changed since then, but the company continues to support other clients like Audi.

My role included but was not limited to: leading and supporting a team of 7 engineers (in house and abroad), a UX IC and two product managers.

In 2013, I took on a new role with Google Maps.

Then What?

From business to consumer, I've faced a number of UX challenges that still scale today

From Google My Business to several framework and feature level changes to Maps, I've learned a lot about scalable frameworks and features across devices, use cases and continents.

I've continued to put users first, while being considerate of of tandem and future brand efforts, as well as ensuring pixel perfection and delight along the way.
