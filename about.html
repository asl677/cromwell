Designing for the masses

Originally from Washington D.C., and living on the west coast for the last several years. From screen printing and lithography to Zippo and Google, I've had the privilege of working on some super impactful projects with some seriously talented players over the years.

How I Got Here

In 2011, I co-founded a business focusing on B2B products, leading nearly all design efforts

We moved to San Francisco to make this all happen.

Known as DarwinApps, I worked with everyone from CMO's to videographers. Typically helping out other early stage products, while focusing on our own ideas at the same time. Clients included The Air National Guard, Creative Dash and AT&T. A few things have changed since then, but the company continues to support other clients like Audi.

My role included but was not limited to: leading and supporting a team of 7 engineers (in house and abroad), a UX IC and two product managers.

In 2013, I was took on a new role with Google Maps.

Then What?

From there, I spent nearly 6 years working on Local Search within Google Maps

From business to consumer, I've faced a number of UX challenges that still scale today. From Google My Business to several framework and feature level changes to Maps,

I've continued to put users first, while being considerate of of tandem and future efforts, as well as ensure pixel perfection and delight along the way.
