Challenge

We needed to brand align + revitalize Navigational Search across Local

We set out to define a short to long term vision - and worked through milestones quarter by quarter.

The crux of this effort stemmed from a company-wide effort to standardize and align design across products. It was a long term vision set out to evolve how Google looks and works across it's ecosystem.

Various parts of Search and Maps needed to adhere and test alongside their specific use cases. Next to Search, Maps and Local was next on the list to re-think their look.

Research

Leverage Search's new design language + cater to top needs like dining, hotels + national parks

Dining, hotels, salons, national parks.

The best path forward was to utilize everything core Search and other areas of Maps were doing - designing a new UI system with ample research involved. Local needed to take on the same principles, which meant stress testing new patterns and suggesting improvements based our specific needs.

Navigational

Refers to when a user lands on a specific place after navigating past an initial local search query, eg 'ABC Cocina or O Cafe on 13th'.

Categorical

This is when a user makes a broad or narrow search query relating to a category, eg 'Ramen nearby' or 'tissue paper CVS'.

Chains

This is more or less as it sounds. A search specifically around a large chain or business, eg 'Target' or 'Starbucks'.

Explorations

This included stress-testing patterns against navigation, imagery + actions across key verticals


We decided to iterate and test on these patterns in order to best accomodate the user needs within iOS, Android and mobile web.

For example, iOS and Android were more focused on driving and overall navigation, rather than core actions like calling and booking a table. Slight variations to our actions and hierarchies needed to be weighed, tested and standardized alongside Search's research and North Star vision.

A places and framework audit was needed to get the ball rolling, so we split that work up between designers already owning various features and work streams.

FINDINGS

Search's patterns needed to be customized to meet Local's needs

These changes and proposals were a tall order, when chasing another org's agenda. While simple in appearance, this was not a simple task to embark on - and wasn't going to happen overnight.

Through extensive user testing and leveraging Search's research team, Local Search was able to iterate on recently modernized elements like actions, tabs, imagery, color and containment models and customize them to fit local's needs and across core verticals.
